## Scene 1: The Watcher

**Setting:** Finn's bedroom, coastal village. Late dusk, the sky a deep violet-blue on the horizon, fading to black overhead.

**Visual description:** A small boy, maybe 9 years old, stands at a window. His palms are pressed flat against the cold glass, fogging it with his breath. Beyond the glass, across a dark stretch of sea, a white lighthouse tower rises from a rocky point. A tiny figure — Old Man Gareth — climbs the spiral stairs visible through the tower's windows. A bell buoy rocks on the tide somewhere in the middle distance, its clang felt more than heard. The room behind the boy is sparse: a bed, a burnt-down fire, a chair. On the table, a half-gutted fish, a knife resting beside it.

**Key narrative beat:** Establishes Finn's nightly ritual of watching, his longing to be part of the lighthouse. Introduces the wound — father lost at sea when the lighthouse was dark. Mother's trauma shown through her avoidance.

**Emotional tone:** Quiet, aching, full of want. The stillness of a child holding his breath.

**Camera direction:** Static wide shot from behind Finn, looking past him through the window. Slow push-in toward the back of his head as the bell buoy sounds. Cut to: close-up of his hands on the glass. Cut to: his reflection layered over the lighthouse.

**Image prompt:** A small boy of about nine stands at a bedroom window at dusk, his palms pressed flat against the cold glass. Beyond the window, across dark water, a white lighthouse rises from a rocky point — a tiny figure climbs its spiral stairs, visible through lit tower windows. A bell buoy floats in the middle distance. The room behind is sparse — a wooden bed, a fireplace with dying embers, a chair, a table with a half-gutted fish and a knife. The boy's breath fogs the glass. His reflection is faintly visible, layered over the lighthouse. Painterly oil painting style, warm interior amber tones contrasting with cool violet-blue exterior, cinematic composition, soft diffused light, melancholic atmosphere, storybook illustration quality, 4K, dramatic lighting.

## Scene 2: The Storm Gathers

**Setting:** The village quay and the base of the lighthouse. Midday, but the sky is the color of a deep bruise — purple-black, swollen.

**Visual description:** The gulls are everywhere on the rooftops, silent, watching the sea. The bell buoy rings faster, frantic. The wind tears a slate from a roof — it crashes on the cobbles below. Laundry tears from a line, spiraling into the gray air. Finn's mother stands in a doorway, her hands raw and red. She is looking at the lighthouse — the first time she has allowed herself to look at it in years. Finn turns to follow her gaze. On the wet stone of the quay, Old Man Gareth staggers, one hand clutching his chest. His face is tilted up toward the dark lantern room. He falls slowly, like a tree dropping. The healer's boy catches him before he hits the ground.

**Key narrative beat:** The catalyst. The storm is both literal and metaphorical. Gareth's collapse forces the village to confront what none of them know. Mother's first look at the lighthouse signals her own healing beginning.

**Emotional tone:** Sudden, violent, disorienting. The world shifting in an instant.

**Camera direction:** Quick cuts. CU of a gull, head cocked. CU of the bell buoy rocking violently. Wide shot of the darkening sky. CU of mother's face — her jaw tight, eyes on the tower she's avoided for years. Slow-motion: Gareth falling, his hand reaching up toward the light that isn't there.

**Image prompt:** A coastal village under a bruise-purple storm sky at midday. Gulls sit silently on rooftops, watching the sea. In the foreground, a woman with red raw hands stands in a doorway, her face tight as she looks across at a white lighthouse tower on a rocky point — the first time she has allowed herself to look at it in years. On the wet stone quay, an old man with a gray beard clutches his chest and falls slowly, his hand reaching toward the dark lantern room of the lighthouse. A slate roof tile lies shattered on the cobbles. Laundry spirals in the wind. Painterly oil painting style, dramatic chiaroscuro, the storm light casting everything in sickly purple-gray, cinematic composition, tension palpable, storybook illustration quality, 4K.

## Scene 3: The Mercy

**Setting:** Deck of the *Mercy*, thirty miles out at sea. Storm. Night falling.

**Visual description:** The ship is being thrown by waves that tower above the mast. Rain comes horizontally, stinging. The topsail has torn off — a shredded remnant whipping in the rigging. The captain is lashed to the wheel, his hands raw and bleeding. Below deck, a girl of about Finn's age, Ellie Thorn, is braced against the cargo, her arms wrapped around wooden crates marked with the healer's cross — medicine. Her knuckles are white. Her eyes are fixed on the dark horizon. She has been watching for hours. There is no light.

**Key narrative beat:** Parallel action. While the village panics, the ship is in genuine peril. Ellie's presence humanizes the stakes — she's not abstract, she's a girl Finn's age who gave him a piece of sea glass.

**Emotional tone:** Cold, brutal, desperate. The vastness of the sea against the smallness of the ship.

**Camera direction:** Low angle from deck looking up at the captain, rain streaking the lens. Wide shot of the wave rising. CU of Ellie's hands gripping the crate, her chapped lips moving — a prayer, a name. CU of the dark horizon — nothing. The absence of light is the most important element in this scene.

**Image prompt:** On the deck of a storm-tossed ship at night, a young girl with red hair braces against wooden crates marked with a healer's cross, her arms wrapped around them, her knuckles white. Rain lashes horizontally, a shredded topsail whips in the rigging above. The captain is lashed to the wheel, his hands bloody and raw. Towering waves rise above the mast, dark and foam-capped. The girl's eyes are fixed on the black horizon — there is no light anywhere. The scene is cold, brutal, desperate. Oil painting style, cool blue-black storm palette, cinematic composition, the small human figures engulfed by the vast indifferent sea, storybook illustration quality, 4K, dramatic storm lighting.

## Scene 4: The Fox & Gull

**Setting:** Interior of the village inn. Night. The storm pounds the roof.

**Visual description:** The Fox & Gull is packed with villagers in oilskins, dripping onto the stone floor where the water hisses and steams against the hearth. The fire throws long shadows up the walls. A dog whines under a table. The blacksmith stands with his hands moving unconsciously — turning an imaginary wheel, testing an imaginary chain. "The gears will strip," he says. "The lamp could shatter." He is afraid, and everyone notices. The innkeeper pours himself a whiskey with a trembling hand. In the corner, pressed against the wall, Finn watches. His heart is visible in the pulse at his throat. His hands are pressed flat against his thighs. We see what he sees in his mind: the memory of his mother's knife stopping, the sea glass under his bed, his father's face fading.

**Key narrative beat:** The village's fear crystallizes around one problem — no one knows the lighthouse. Finn's internal debate: he knows the mechanism but is afraid of the cost of being wrong.

**Emotional tone:** Claustrophobic, panicked, the weight of collective fear pressing in.

**Camera direction:** Steadicam moving through the crowd — faces, wet wool, steaming breath. A slow orbit around Finn in the corner, isolating him from the noise. Flash frames between present and memory: the knife stopping, the sea glass, Gareth's hands turning the wheel.

**Image prompt:** A crowded fishing inn interior at night, packed with villagers in dripping oilskins standing around a stone hearth where wet clothes hiss and steam on the fire. Long shadows crawl up the rough walls. A brown dog whines beneath a wooden table. A large blacksmith in a leather apron stands with his hands unconsciously turning an imaginary wheel, his face afraid. The innkeeper pours whiskey with a trembling hand. In a dark corner, a small boy presses against the pine wall — his eyes wide, his hands flat against his thighs, his pulse visible at his throat. Warm amber firelight contrasting with deep shadow. Oil painting style, Rembrandt-esque chiaroscuro, claustrophobic composition, the crowd pressing in around the isolated boy, storybook illustration quality, 4K.

## Scene 5: The Choice

**Setting:** Outside the inn, then the base of the lighthouse. Night, storm at full force.

**Visual description:** The inn door groans shut behind Finn, cutting off the warm yellow light. He is alone in the rain — it hits him like a wall, horizontal, stinging. He runs, bare feet on wet cobbles. At the base of the lighthouse, he stops. The tower looms, black against black. The bell buoy rings frantic. He looks up at the dark lantern room — the glass is dead, reflecting nothing. He puts his hand on the cold wet stone of the first step. Then he stops. His foot comes back down. He turns. The Fox & Gull glows yellow through the rain. Shapes move behind the windows. He can go back. He can be a boy. Then — his father's voice, clear and close: *But the light won't light itself.* He turns back to the stairs.

**Key narrative beat:** The turning point. Finn's hand on the cold stone is the physical manifestation of his decision. The moment he turns back, hears his father's voice, and chooses to climb — this is the heart of his arc.

**Emotional tone:** The rawest point of the story. Fear, loneliness, memory, and the quiet courage of a child who chooses to act.

**Camera direction:** Extreme wide shot — small boy, massive tower. Then CU of his hand touching the wet stone. CU of his face, rain streaming, as he turns. SFX: the father's voice is just audible over the storm. Then a single CU of Finn's foot stepping onto the first stair — the hardest step he will ever take.

**Image prompt:** A small boy in a nightshirt stands at the base of a towering lighthouse during a violent storm, rain lashing horizontally, his bare feet on wet cobblestones. He has one hand on the cold wet stone of the first step but has stopped — his foot has come back down, and he has turned to look back over his shoulder at a warm yellow-lit inn across the square, shapes moving behind its windows. Rain streams down his face. The lighthouse looms above him, black against the black sky, its lantern room dark and dead. A bell buoy rings frantically in the distance. The storm-tossed sea crashes behind him. Extreme wide shot emphasizing how small the boy is against the tower. Oil painting style, dramatic chiaroscuro, the warm yellow of the inn contrasting with the cold blue-black storm, cinematic composition, storybook illustration, 4K.

## Scene 6: The Climb

**Setting:** The spiral staircase inside the lighthouse tower. Dark, narrow, stone.

**Visual description:** Finn climbs. The stairs wind in tight spirals, each step worn smooth and slightly concave by decades of feet. He passes the first landing — a small window shows the village below, tiny yellow squares of light, the dark shape of the inn. He keeps climbing. The second landing shows the ocean — white-capped and heaving, the bell buoy tossed like a toy. Halfway up, the smell changes: salt and stone give way to oil and brass and old dust. The wind screams through hairline cracks in the mortar, a thin high whistle. The stone vibrates under his hand — the whole tower sways with each gust. His wet clothes cling to him, heavy and cold. His teeth chatter. He bites his tongue — copper and salt. He wants to stop. He wants to sit down. He keeps climbing.

**Key narrative beat:** The physical ordeal of the climb mirrors his internal journey. Each landing is a milestone. The changing smell signals he is entering a different world — the domain of the mechanism.

**Emotional tone:** Physical struggle, growing determination, the body pushing past its limits.

**Camera direction:** Low angle looking up the spiral stairs, Finn's legs appearing and disappearing as he climbs. CU of his hand on the worn stone wall. CU of his wet bare feet on the concave steps. Each landing as a brief pause — a window showing the world shrinking below. The sound design is crucial: his breathing, the whistle of wind, the slap of wet feet on stone.

**Image prompt:** Inside a narrow spiral stone staircase winding upward in tight circles. A small boy in a wet nightshirt climbs the worn steps, one hand on the curving stone wall. The steps are smooth and slightly concave from decades of use. At a small arched window on a landing, the view shows a tiny village below with yellow-lit windows and the dark shape of an inn. Higher up, another landing window shows a raging white-capped sea and a bell buoy being tossed like a toy. The tower walls are old stone with hairline cracks. The boy's wet clothes cling to him, his hair plastered. Muted lantern light casts long shadows. Low angle looking up the spiral making the climb seem endless. Oil painting style, warm stone tones transitioning to cooler brass-and-oil tones higher up, cinematic composition, storybook illustration, 4K.

## Scene 7: The Mechanism

**Setting:** The lantern room at the top of the lighthouse. Night. Absolute silence after the storm's noise.

**Visual description:** The heavy oak door swings open into a silence so deep it seems to press against the ears. The mechanism waits — a complex array of brass valves green with patina, dark glass prisms that catch no light, a great lamp dead and cold. The room smells of kerosene and old smoke. Finn crosses to the wheel — his fingers find the familiar grooves, the spot where the brass is worn thinner, the slight wobble in the handle. Forty years of Gareth's hands have shaped this metal. Finn closes his eyes. We see a flash of Gareth's hands turning the same wheel. When Finn opens his eyes, he begins to turn. *One.* The wheel resists — he puts his whole weight into it. *Two.* It gives with a groan deep in the wall. *Three.* It turns freely, gears engaging. He pulls the chain — slow and steady — feels the shutters click open above, hears the hiss of gas begin to flow.

**Key narrative beat:** The physical connection between Finn and Gareth through the mechanism. Finn's knowledge, built through watching, becomes action. The room is a cathedral of brass and glass.

**Emotional tone:** Reverent, tense, mechanical ritual. The silence is holy.

**Camera direction:** Slow push-in through the doorway as it opens. CU of the brass wheel — the worn grooves, the patina. CU of Finn's fingers tracing the wear marks. Intercut with flash-frames of Gareth's hands doing the same. Slow-motion: the wheel turning, the chain pulling, the shutters opening. The hiss of gas is the first sound to break the silence.

**Image prompt:** A small lantern room at the top of a lighthouse, filled with an intricate brass mechanism — valves green with patina, dark glass prisms, a great dead lamp rising in the center. The room smells of kerosene and old smoke. A young boy in a wet nightshirt stands before a large brass wheel, his small hands on the worn grooves. The brass is worn thinner in one spot, the handle slightly loose from decades of use. He is beginning to turn the wheel, his whole weight leaning into it. In the background, a heavy oak door stands open. The room is dark except for faint storm light through the glass. Reverent, silent, cathedral-like atmosphere. Oil painting style, deep shadows with brass highlights, green patina tones against dark wood, cinematic composition, storybook illustration, 4K.

## Scene 8: The Light

**Setting:** The lantern room. Same moment as Scene 7, continuing.

**Visual description:** Finn takes the match from the tin box. His fingers are numb and clumsy. He holds the match — not yet striking. The pause. This is where Gareth would whisper something. Finn doesn't know the words, so he says his own: *For the ships. For the children. For my father.* He strikes the match against the stone sill. The flame flares blue — chemical sharp — then steadies into gold. He opens the valve. The mantles catch: first a dim orange glow, then a blazing white heat that blooms, filling the room with sudden brightness. The prisms catch the light and throw it outward — a long blade of gold cutting across the storm. *Tick.* The mechanism engages. *Tick.* The lamp rotates. *Tick.* The beam sweeps across the water and finds the horizon. Finn puts his hands on the warm brass and feels it humming — the heartbeat of the lighthouse, alive again. The warmth spreads up his arms. The light catches dust in the air, filling the room with tiny stars, gleaming on the green patina, turning it to gold. Finn stands in the middle of it and for the first time since his father died, he is not cold.

**Key narrative beat:** The climax. The moment of creation. Finn's internal transformation made manifest in light. The lamp is more than a lamp — it is the thing his father didn't have, the thing Finn now provides for others.

**Emotional tone:** Awe, triumph, warmth bursting through cold. The visual peak of the film.

**Camera direction:** CU of the match striking, blue flame. CU of Finn's face as the light blooms — the orange-gold illumination rising across his features. Slow-motion: the mantles catching, the prisms igniting. The camera pulls back as the beam shoots outward — a visual commitment to the light. Final shot: Finn's hands on the warm brass, the dust motes like stars around him. The *tick* of the mechanism is the only sound.

**Image prompt:** The lantern room of a lighthouse illuminated by a blazing golden light. A small boy stands before the great lamp, which has just ignited — the mantles glow white-hot, the prisms catch the light and throw it outward in a long golden beam through the storm beyond the glass. The room is filled with floating dust motes that look like tiny stars in the golden light. The boy's hands rest on the warm brass mechanism, which gleams with reflected gold, the green patina transformed. His face is lit from within, warm and awestruck. The brass wheel shows worn grooves from decades of use. Dust particles float in the air like constellations. Oil painting style, warm gold tones dominating, the moment of creation and warmth bursting through cold darkness, cinematic composition, storybook illustration, 4K, radiant lighting.

## Scene 9: The Sight

**Setting:** Deck of the *Mercy*. Storm still raging, but the light has appeared.

**Visual description:** Ellie is still braced against the crates, her eyes on the horizon. Then — a point of gold. Small. Steady. She doesn't breathe. The beam sweeps toward them, cutting a path through the rain and spray. "Light!" she screams. The captain turns the wheel. The crew pulls on the lines. The *Mercy* follows the golden path through the dark. Ellie watches the tower emerge from the storm, the light growing larger. She thinks of the boy with the quiet eyes who put a piece of sea glass in his pocket and said thank you like he really meant it.

**Key narrative beat:** The consequence of Finn's choice. The light saves them. Ellie's recognition that the light came from someone she knows — it's personal, not abstract.

**Emotional tone:** Hope breaking through despair. Relief so intense it's almost painful.

**Camera direction:** CU of Ellie's face — exhausted, cold, defeated. Then the reflection of the light appears in her eyes before she sees it consciously — a tiny gold point in her pupils. Then she turns and sees it. The scream is raw. High angle: the *Mercy* turning, the beam of gold cutting across the dark water like a road. CU of Ellie's hands releasing the crates.

**Image prompt:** On the rain-lashed deck of a storm-battered ship at night, a young girl with red hair braces against wooden crates. A golden beam of light sweeps across the sea toward her, cutting a path through the rain and spray, reflecting on the dark water like a road of gold. The girl's face is lit by the approaching light — her eyes wide, her chapped lips parting in astonishment. Behind her, a bearded captain struggles with the wheel, turning the ship to follow the beam. Crew members pull on ropes. The lighthouse tower is visible in the far distance, a tiny bright point growing larger. Oil painting style, dramatic contrast between the cold blue-black storm and the warm golden beam cutting through it, cinematic composition, storybook illustration, 4K.

## Scene 10: The Embrace

**Setting:** The lantern room. Storm still audible but muffled, distant.

**Visual description:** Footsteps on the stairs. The blacksmith enters first, face pale in the lamplight. Then the healer. Then Finn's mother. She stands in the doorway, not entering. She looks at Finn — a small boy in the middle of all that light. Then she crosses the room and kneels beside him and puts her arms around him. Her body shakes. She presses her face into his wet hair. Outside, the storm rages. Inside, the lamp turns, ticks, fills the room with gold. "Your father would be proud," she whispers. "He would be so proud." Finn doesn't speak. He leans into her. The warmth is on both of them.

**Key narrative beat:** The emotional resolution of the mother's arc. She entered the lighthouse. She touched her son inside the light. She spoke about his father openly, without flinching. She is healing.

**Emotional tone:** The quietest moment of the film. Tenderness, release, family.

**Camera direction:** The shot through the doorway — mother's silhouette against the light, Finn small within it. Slow push-in as she crosses the room. CU of her hands around him. CU of his face, eyes closed, leaning into her. The lamp ticks in the background. The camera slowly rises to a high angle looking down at them — two figures in a circle of gold.

**Image prompt:** Inside a golden-lit lighthouse lantern room, a woman kneels on the worn wooden floor with her arms wrapped around a small boy in a wet nightshirt. She presses her face into his wet hair, her body shaking. The great lamp above them turns slowly, casting warm golden light, the brass gleaming. Dust motes float like tiny stars. A large blacksmith stands in the doorway in silhouette, watching. The storm is visible through the glass — rain streaking, but muffled, distant. The scene is intimate, tender, a circle of warmth and gold in the dark. Oil painting style, warm amber and gold tones, the mother and child illuminated as if by a hearth fire, cinematic composition, storybook illustration, 4K.

## Scene 11: The Dawn

**Setting:** The quay and the lighthouse, morning after the storm. First light.

**Visual description:** The storm has passed. The sea is flat and gray, almost apologetic. The *Mercy* rounds the point — her sails in rags, her hull scarred, but alive. She drops anchor. Finn watches from the lantern room as a small figure with red hair runs down the gangplank and crashes into her father's arms. The healer closes his eyes and holds his daughter. On the quay below, Finn's mother stands looking up at the tower. She raises her hand — not a wave, just a sign that she sees him. Finn presses his palm flat against the cool glass of the lantern room. He is inside the light now.

**Key narrative beat:** The resolution. Ellie is safe. The ship is safe. The mother has come to the quay to look at the lighthouse freely. Finn's position inside the light completes his transformation from watcher to keeper.

**Emotional tone:** Quiet triumph, restoration, the peace after the storm.

**Camera direction:** Wide shot of the *Mercy* rounding the point — the ruined sails, the golden light from the tower still glowing in the dawn. Slow-motion: Ellie running, the collision with her father. CU of Finn's mother raising her hand. CU of Finn through the glass — his palm pressed flat, the same gesture as Scene 1 but now he is inside.

**Image prompt:** Morning after a storm at a coastal village. A scarred sailing ship with torn sails rounds a rocky point in flat gray light, dropping anchor in a calm sea. Through the lantern room window high above, a young boy presses his palm flat against the glass, looking down. The tower's light still glows faintly in the gray dawn. Oil painting style, muted gray-gold dawn palette, quiet restoration after violence, cinematic composition, storybook illustration, 4K.

## Scene 12: The Inheritance

**Setting:** The lantern room. Three days after the storm. Clear daylight.

**Visual description:** Old Man Gareth climbs the stairs, one at a time, pausing at each landing to catch his breath. At the top, he doesn't speak. He stands and looks at the mechanism. Then he runs his fingers over the wheel — the way a man touches a sleeping child, the way a man says goodbye without words. "You found the pause," he says. "Before the match. You found it yourself." He sits on the worn step. After a moment, Finn sits beside him. "I never had children," Gareth says. "This tower was my child. I talked to it. I worried about it. I loved it." He looks at Finn. "And now it's yours." He puts his hand on Finn's shoulder — the same hand that has turned the wheel ten thousand times. "Tomorrow night, you light it alone. I'll be watching."

**Key narrative beat:** The formal passing of responsibility. Gareth's confession — the tower was his child — is the deepest moment of intimacy he has shown. Finn's silent acceptance is his maturation.

**Emotional tone:** Bittersweet, gentle, the weight of legacy lightened by love.

**Camera direction:** Medium two-shot of Gareth and Finn on the step. CU of Gareth's hand on the wheel — tracing the worn brass. CU of Finn's face as he listens — he is no longer a boy in this moment. Wide shot of them sitting together, the mechanism around them, the daylight streaming through the prisms.

**Image prompt:** Inside a lighthouse lantern room on a clear day, an old man with a gray beard and hands like rope sits on a worn wooden step beside a young boy of about twelve. The old man has one hand on the young boy's shoulder — his other hand rests on a large brass wheel, his fingers tracing the worn grooves. His eyes are on the mechanism, his expression gentle and bittersweet. The boy sits close, looking at the old man, his face serious and older than his years. Sunlight streams through the glass prisms, casting rainbow fragments across the floor. The brass gleams warmly. The scene is intimate, the passing of a legacy. Oil painting style, warm daylight tones, soft shadows, the feeling of a sacred moment between two people, cinematic composition, storybook illustration, 4K.

## Scene 13: The Years

**Setting:** The lighthouse and village, changing across seasons. Montage.

**Visual description:** A sequence of moments over four years:
- Winter: Finn alone in the lantern room, snow against the glass, his hands on the warm brass.
- Spring: Finn oiling the gears, his hands now confident and practiced, the tower opening like a living thing around him.
- Summer: Finn on the roof of the lighthouse, looking out at a flat blue sea. He knows the constellations now — he names them aloud in the dark.
- Autumn: Finn fixing the crack in the mortar, his hands sure on the trowel.
- Winter again: The cold seeping through the stone. Finn replacing a mantle, the delicate mesh crumbling like old lace between his fingers.
- Evening: Finn looks down from the lantern room. His mother stands at the bedroom window — the same window where he used to stand. She does not press her palms to the glass. She just stands there, her hand on the sill, watching the light.

**Key narrative beat:** Finn grows into his role. The lighthouse becomes not just a job but a relationship. His mother's healing is shown through her watching — she has taken his former position.

**Emotional tone:** Cyclical, patient, the slow work of time. The warmth of growing competence.

**Camera direction:** Transition shots between seasons — the same window, the same tower, the same hands, but the world outside changes. Match cuts: Finn's hands as a boy on the glass → Finn's hands as a young man on the brass → mother's hand on the windowsill. Each shot held just long enough to feel time passing.

## Scene 14: The Apprentice

**Setting:** The base of the lighthouse tower. Four winters after the storm. Rain, not storm, but steady rain.

**Visual description:** A boy — maybe eight years old, dark hair plastered to his forehead — stands at the base of the tower. He is looking up at the lantern room. He does not move. He has the same hungry look in his eyes that Finn remembers having. Finn watches from the top of the stairs. Then he walks down. "What's your name?" "Tom. Tom Sparrow." The boy's voice is small but steady. "I want to learn the light." Finn looks at him. The boy does not look away. Finn smiles — the same smile Gareth gave him, years ago. The smile that means *I see you. I was you. Wait.* "You've been watching," Finn says. Tom nods. "Yes, sir. Every night." Finn puts his hand on the boy's shoulder — the same spot where Gareth's hand was, where his mother's hand was. "Come on, then. The first lesson is watching."

**Key narrative beat:** The cycle continues. Finn becomes Gareth. Tom becomes Finn. The scene is a deliberate echo of the opening — the watcher at the base, the keeper at the top.

**Emotional tone:** Recognition, the sweetness of return, the gentle humor of seeing yourself in someone else.

**Camera direction:** High angle from the tower looking down at Tom — small and still, exactly as Finn was. Reverse: low angle from Tom's perspective looking up at Finn. CU of Finn's smile — the recognition, the echo. CU of Finn's hand on Tom's shoulder — matching exactly the shot of Gareth's hand on Finn's shoulder from Scene 12.

**Image prompt:** At the base of a white lighthouse tower on a rainy evening, a young boy of about eight with dark hair plastered to his forehead stands looking up at the lantern room. His clothes are wet from steady rain. He has the same hungry, determined look in his eyes as someone who has been watching for a long time. # Visual Themes & Motifs

## Running Visual Themes

### 1. Hands on Glass (Watching → Being Inside)

| Scene | Who | On What | Position |
|-------|-----|---------|----------|
| 1 | Finn (age 9) | Bedroom window | Looking out at lighthouse |
| 11 | Finn (age 9) | Lantern room glass | Looking down from inside the light |
| 13 | Mother | Bedroom windowsill | Standing where Finn once stood |
| 15 | Tom (age 8) | Lantern room glass | Inside the light, beginning Finn's journey |

The dominant visual metaphor: hands on glass moves from **outside wanting in** to **inside providing the light**. The composition stays identical — a boy at a window at dusk, palms flat, breath fogging — but the context transforms from longing to belonging.

### 2. Hands on the Brass Wheel (Knowledge → Mastery → Legacy)

- **Scene 7**: Finn's child hands on the wheel — tentative, tracing grooves Gareth made
- **Scene 8**: Finn's hands on warm brass — the moment of creation, the heat of the living lamp
- **Scene 12**: Gareth's fingers tracing the wheel — farewell, recognizing what forty years of touch have shaped
- **Scene 15**: Finn's hand on the wheel — confident, teaching, the master now

### 3. Hand on Shoulder (The Unbroken Chain)

- **Scene 12**: Gareth's hand on Finn's shoulder — "This tower was my child. Now it's yours."
- **Scene 14**: Finn's hand on Tom's shoulder — "The first lesson is watching."

### 4. The Bell Buoy (Anchoring Sound of the Story)

Present in scenes 1, 2, 5, 6, 9, 13, 15. Its character changes with the story:
- **Calm**: rocking peacefully, clang felt more than heard
- **Crisis**: ringing faster, frantic, desperate, tossed like a toy
- **Resolution**: ringing in the dusk, calm again

### 5. Gold vs. Cold (Warmth of Light vs. Absence)

The color temperature contrast runs through every scene:
- **Cold**: the blue-black storm, dark glass, dead lamp, the sea, the father's absence
- **Gold**: the lamp, the brass, the inn windows, the match flame, the beam, the mother's arms
- **Climax** (Scene 8): "For the first time since his father died, he was not cold" — the gold finally conquers the cold

### 6. The Beam of Light (The Central Image)

- **Scene 1**: Finn watches it from outside — he is not part of it
- **Scene 8**: Finn creates it — it comes from his hands
- **Scene 9**: The ship follows it — it saves lives
- **Scene 11**: It glows in the dawn — it has done its work
- **Scene 13**: The mother watches it — she is healed
- **Scene 15**: It sweeps out again — it will never stop
